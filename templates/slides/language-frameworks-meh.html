Instead, we need to approach our broader challenges as libraries, museums, archives, and communities that care about our past, current, and future culture stores, through a more inclusive and compelling, narrative-driven, iterative technology I call a Catalog Pull Platform - an open-source vision of library technology that is owned and shared for us and by us for our institutions and people.

What do people (maybe) understand?

I've found that most people understand what is a library catalog, anything else and confusion sets in. The hype and promise of the semantic web - and library linked-data linked data in particular - is that I shouldn't care about the how only you publish linked data, only that it is available for others to use and reuse.
