BIBFRAME Works & Instances

Using the Semantic Server has allowed me to experiment with different Linked-Data vocabularies but given my interest on library data, I've been mostly interested in two; schema.org and BIBFRAME, with most of my development work for the past few years focusing on the BIBFRAME, the Library of Congress replacement for MARC.

In the BIBFRAME Datastore, RDF graphs for Works and Instances are broken out into subject graphs that are ingested into Fedora, Fuseki, and then indexed in Elasticsearch. Work is defined as "As an abstract representation of the conceptual essence of a resource". Here is partial example of BIBFRAME Work for Lewis Carroll's Alice in Wonderland:
Work URI Predicate Object
http://catalog.coloradocollege.edu/3417443 rdf:type bf:Text
bf:Work
bf:authorizedAccessPoint "Carroll, Lewis, 1832-1898. Alice's adventures in wonderland :and Through the looking-glass"
bf:creator http://catalog.coloradocollege.edu/3417443person6
bf:language http://id.loc.gov/vocabulary/languages/eng

Instance URI Predicate Object
http://catalog.coloradocollege.edu/3417443instance13 rdf:type bf:Instance
bf:Monograph
bf:providerStatement "New York : Illustrated Editions Co. 19--?"
bf:instanceOf http://catalog.coloradocollege.edu/3417443
